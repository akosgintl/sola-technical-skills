# Cloud-Native Patterns

> [!summary]
> The architectural patterns that exploit cloud elasticity and managed services — event-driven designs, serverless, and container orchestration — to build scalable, resilient systems.

**Domain:** [[tier-1-edge|Cloud Architecture]]

## What it is

Cloud-native patterns are the design idioms suited to dynamic, distributed cloud platforms: loosely coupled services communicating via events, compute that scales to zero, and workloads packaged in containers and orchestrated declaratively. They favor managed services, horizontal scaling, and designing for failure over lifting-and-shifting traditional architectures.

## Key concepts

- Event-driven architecture
- Serverless architecture
- Kubernetes and container orchestration
- Managed services and scale-to-zero
- Designing for failure and elasticity
