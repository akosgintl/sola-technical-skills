# Dynamic / Surge Team-Staffing Models

> [!summary]
> Dynamic team-staffing is the emerging practice of assembling specialist capacity on demand — rather than maintaining permanently staffed teams — by combining human experts with agentic AI workflows. Agentic systems can handle routine delivery tasks at scale, freeing organizations to bring in specialist humans briefly for high-judgment work. The model is enabled by AI that can onboard context quickly and operate autonomously between human touchpoints; it shifts org design from headcount-centric to outcome-centric.

**Domain:** [[tier-3-watch|Emerging & Adjacent]]

## What it is

Traditional software teams are permanent, cross-functional squads (the "Spotify model"). Dynamic staffing flips this: core teams are lean and own architecture + judgment, while delivery surges are handled by a mix of AI agents (writing code, running tests, generating reports) and short-engagement specialists (security auditors, ML engineers, compliance reviewers) brought in for bounded tasks. Agentic systems provide the continuity and throughput; humans provide the judgment and accountability gates.

## Key concepts / building blocks

- **Agentic throughput layer** — AI agents handle volume tasks (code generation, testing, documentation, PR review)
- **Human judgment gates** — bounded specialist engagements for architecture review, compliance sign-off, security audits
- **Context-as-a-service** — the wiki / knowledge base that onboards specialists quickly, replacing tribal knowledge
- **Team Topologies (Skelton & Pais)** — stream-aligned, platform, enabling, complicated-subsystem team patterns
- **Staff augmentation vs. dynamic staffing** — the former maintains headcount; the latter scales event-driven

## Pitfalls & anti-patterns

- Treating AI agents as permanent headcount replacements; ignoring accountability gaps
- No knowledge capture between specialist engagements (context lost on exit)
- Dynamic staffing without governance: who owns decisions made by transient teams?
- Using surge models for high-continuity work (platform ownership, incident response) where context depth matters
