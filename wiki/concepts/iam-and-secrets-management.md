# IAM and Secrets Management

> [!summary]
> The disciplines of managing who (and what) can access resources and of securely storing, rotating, and distributing credentials such as keys, tokens, and certificates.

**Domain:** [[tier-1-edge|Security & Compliance]]

## What it is

Identity and access management governs authentication and authorization for users, services, and increasingly agents, expressing permissions through roles and policies. Secrets management handles the credentials themselves — vaulting them, rotating them, issuing short-lived tokens, and keeping them out of code. Together they are the backbone of least-privilege access and a precondition for zero trust.

## Key concepts

- Authentication and authorization
- Roles, policies, and least privilege
- Secret vaulting and rotation
- Short-lived / dynamic credentials
- Workload and non-human identity
