# Agent Governance and Policy

> [!summary]
> The rules, allow-lists, and audit mechanisms that constrain what agents may do and create a defensible record of what they did — the policy layer for autonomous actors.

**Domain:** [[tier-1-edge|AI & Agentic Architecture]]

## What it is

Agent governance defines the policies that bound agent behavior: which tools and data are allow-listed, which actions require approval, and what must be logged. It pairs preventive controls (allow-lists, guardrails) with detective controls (audit trails) so agent activity is both constrained and reviewable. Increasingly this is expressed as policy-as-code rather than prose.

## Key concepts

- Allow-lists and deny-lists for tools/actions
- Audit trails and traceability
- Policy-as-code enforcement
- Approval and escalation policies
- Compliance alignment
