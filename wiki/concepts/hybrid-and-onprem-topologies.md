# Hybrid and On-Prem Topologies

> [!summary]
> Architectures that combine public cloud with on-premises or edge infrastructure, driven by data gravity, latency, sovereignty, and existing investment.

**Domain:** [[tier-1-edge|Cloud Architecture]]

## What it is

Hybrid and on-prem topologies blend cloud elasticity with the reality that some data and workloads stay on owned infrastructure or at the edge. Drivers include data gravity (moving large datasets is costly), low-latency or disconnected operation, regulatory sovereignty, and sunk hardware investment. The architecture must keep identity, networking, and operations coherent across the boundary.

## Key concepts

- Data gravity and locality
- Edge computing topologies
- Connectivity and network design
- Sovereignty and regulatory constraints
- Consistent identity and operations across boundaries
