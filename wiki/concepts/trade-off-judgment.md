# Trade-off Judgment

> [!summary]
> The architect's core competency: making and defending decisions that balance cost, risk, scale, time, and complexity when there is no objectively correct answer.

**Priority:** 🔴 P0 · **Domain:** [[meta-skills|Meta-Skills]] · **Roadmap:** §M.2

## What it is

Trade-off judgment is the ability to weigh competing forces — cost versus resilience, speed versus quality, flexibility versus simplicity — and choose a defensible position given the context. It includes naming the trade-off explicitly, surfacing assumptions, and documenting why an option was chosen over alternatives. This judgment, not knowledge of any single technology, is what distinguishes a senior architect.

## Key concepts

- Cost vs. risk vs. scale vs. time
- Naming and documenting trade-offs
- Context-dependence of "right" answers
- Reversible vs. irreversible decisions
- Architecture decision records
