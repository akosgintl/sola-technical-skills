# Accountable Human Layer

> [!summary]
> The principle that a named human remains responsible and answerable for automated and AI-driven decisions — the locus of accountability that machines cannot hold.

**Priority:** 🔴 P0 · **Domain:** [[meta-skills|Meta-Skills]] · **Roadmap:** §M.4

## What it is

The accountable human layer is the insistence that, however autonomous a system becomes, accountability ultimately rests with identifiable people. Agents and models can act, but they cannot be answerable to regulators, customers, or boards. Designing systems with clear human ownership, sign-off, and traceability keeps responsibility from evaporating into automation.

## Key concepts

- Named human ownership of outcomes
- Accountability vs. automation
- Sign-off and approval responsibility
- Traceability to a responsible party
- Regulatory and ethical answerability
