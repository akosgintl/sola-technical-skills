# Systems Thinking over Syntax

> [!summary]
> The senior architect's bias toward understanding how parts interact, where the constraints and failure modes are, and what the second-order effects of a design are — rather than memorizing the syntax of any one tool.

**Priority:** 🔴 P0 · **Domain:** [[meta-skills|Meta-Skills]] · **Roadmap:** §M.1

## What it is

Systems thinking is the habit of reasoning about a whole system — its components, interactions, feedback loops, and emergent behavior — instead of optimizing one part in isolation. As AI generates more syntax, the durable value moves to seeing boundaries, dependencies, failure modes, and second-order consequences. It is the meta-skill that makes every domain skill compound.

## Key concepts

- Interactions and feedback loops over components
- Constraints, bottlenecks, and failure modes
- Second-order effects of design choices
- Boundaries and dependencies
- Emergent behavior in distributed systems
